Your Name

A hundred people know your name. About six know you're hanging by a thread. A thousand see you break in the bus, in the pharmacy with a coffee spill on your blouse - they do not know your name but know everything else. Your shadow smears the pavement. Your exhale lingers between branches, within leaves. Summary:

The poem poetically captures the essence of being seen yet unseen, known yet unknown, in the midst of everyday life and natural surroundings. It suggests that while our presence may fade from memory, our impact cannot be erased, echoing the enduring influence of our existence.

Explanation:

The poem explores the contrast between how people are known or seen by others versus the deeper, more personal experiences and struggles they face internally. It highlights the dichotomy between public perception and private reality.

Notes:

  • Visibility vs. Invisibility:
    The poem discusses how many people may know your name or recognize you superficially, but very few understand your true emotional state or the challenges you face.


  • Daily Life:
    It touches on mundane yet significant moments like spilling coffee or experiencing personal struggles in public spaces, contrasting them with the anonymity of the individual involved.


  • Nature Imagery:
    The use of natural elements like shadows, breath, sun, and moon evokes a sense of connection between personal experiences and the larger universe.
