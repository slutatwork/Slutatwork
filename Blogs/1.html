Alive

“ You are alive, I whisper to myself, therefore something in you listens.” ― Ilya Kaminsky, [Deaf Republic]

Summary:

This quote from Ilya Kaminsky’s Deaf Republic encapsulates a moment of introspection and self-affirmation.
It suggests that the act of being alive inherently involves an inner awareness or consciousness that listens and
responds to our inner thoughts and feelings.

Explanation and Notes:

Introspection and Self-Affirmation:

The quote reveals a quiet moment of self-acknowledgment.
By whispering to oneself, it shows an intimate conversation happening internally.
It's a reminder that despite any external silence or deafness, there is an inner voice and listener within each of us.


The Essence of Being Alive:

To be alive means more than just physical existence.
It includes a deeper awareness and the capability to listen to one's own thoughts and emotions.
This internal dialogue is a testament to one's existence and humanity.


Inner Awareness:

The phrase "something in you listens" points to a profound level of self-awareness.
It's an acknowledgment that even in silence, there is a part of us that is attentive and responsive.
This inner listener is a crucial aspect of our being, signifying that we are not just passive
beings but are actively engaging with our inner world.


Resilience and Hope:

On a more profound level, this quote can be seen as a message of resilience.
Despite any struggles or feelings of isolation, whispering to oneself and recognizing
that there is something that listens within can be a source of comfort and hope.


My Perspective:

When I reflect on Kaminsky’s words, I see them as a powerful affirmation of
my inner strength and consciousness. It’s a reminder that even in my quietest moments,
when it feels like the world isn’t listening, there’s a part of me that is always paying attention.
This inner listener is a source of resilience, guiding me through times of doubt and reinforcing my sense of being.
This quote encourages me to trust in my inner voice, to find solace in my own presence,
and to recognize the profound significance of being alive.
